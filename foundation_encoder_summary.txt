Foundation Model Encoder Swap: EMoDB + AIBO Results
====================================================

Swapped the audio encoder in the linear/MLP probe pipeline
(baselines/embedding_probes.py) for the raw audio_tower output of two
full audio-language models, instead of the SSL encoders (wav2vec2,
WavLM, HuBERT) used before:

  - Qwen2-Audio-7B     (Qwen/Qwen2-Audio-7B)
  - Audio Flamingo 3   (nvidia/audio-flamingo-3-hf)

Both are Whisper-style encoders (32 layers, 1280-dim), used pre-projection
so directly comparable to the SSL encoders. Tested on EMoDB and AIBO with
the same speaker-independent splits as the AudioGPT2 experiments.

Both LALM encoders beat every WavLM-based system on accuracy and WF1, but
both underperform AudioGPT2 on UAR -- the metric that matters most given the
65% neutral imbalance. Per-class recall shows why:

Class        AudioGPT2(WavLM)  qwen2-audio(lin)  audio-flamingo-3(lin)
anger              56.5             60.4                47.0
emphatic           47.9             32.7                42.2
neutral            57.8             67.8                72.7
positive           48.4             44.7                49.3
rest               17.0              7.7                 3.7

Both LALM encoders separate "neutral" better than WavLM+AudioGPT2, but are
much worse on "rest" specifically (3.7-7.7% recall vs. 17.0%) -- consistent
across two different vendors' encoders, so this looks like a real,
structural weakness on this heterogeneous catch-all class, not noise.


Padding / file size
----------------------
Full (non-pooled) per-frame embeddings would be ~65GB per encoder per
dataset on AIBO (18,216 samples x 750 frames x 1280 dim x 4 bytes), vs.
~17GB for the existing WavLM embeddings. The 750-frame figure is fixed
regardless of clip length -- AIBO's median clip is 1.6s, but the
Whisper-style feature extractor pads every clip to the same internal window
either way.

Tested whether this is avoidable
(interpretability/check_variable_length_encoding.py): fed both encoders
real AIBO clips with padding disabled at the feature-extractor level. Both
crash with a fixed-shape mismatch as soon as the input isn't padded to the
model's configured max length (max_source_positions=1500) -- confirmed
separately for Qwen2-Audio and Audio Flamingo 3 (different error message,
same root cause: a fixed-size assumption in the encoder itself, not
something exposed through the feature extractor's padding option).

So: extracted mean-pooled embeddings only for AIBO (93MB each, sufficient
for the linear/MLP probes above), not the full 65GB per-frame version.


Cost of full AudioGPT2 fusion training on these encoders (not attempted)
----------------------------------------------------------------------------
Would need full (non-pooled) embeddings per encoder per dataset (~65GB,
~9-10h extraction each on AIBO), then a new AudioGPT2 training run per
encoder on top of that. Both encoders on AIBO: ~130GB disk, ~18-20 GPU
hours for extraction alone, before any training.


Interpretation
-----------------
Independent evidence for the same conclusion as the logit-lens analysis:
representation quality dominates over the fusion architecture. Logit-lens
found AudioGPT2's cross-attention adapters contribute ~0 KL to the
classifier's belief; this experiment shows a strong-enough encoder with
just a linear/MLP head matches or beats the full fusion architecture on
EMoDB, and comes close on AIBO except for the "rest"-class gap.

Zhao et al.'s neuron-level ESN paper (emotion control in generative LALMs)
found the structural mirror of this on a different task: emotion-control
signal concentrates in LM-side decoder MLPs, while the audio encoder and
synthesis modules are comparatively inert. Two different methods, two
different tasks, same broad claim about where emotion-relevant computation
lives in audio-LLM systems.
